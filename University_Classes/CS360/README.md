# CS360
Portfolio Submission for CS360 Mobile Architect and Programming

## Briefly summarize the requirements and goals of the app you developed. What user needs was this app designed to address? ##

The goal of the Blackburn Weight Tracker app was to create a simple and intuitive Android application that would allow users to log daily weight entries and track their progress over time. The app was designed to address the need for consistency and ease of use by minimizing unnecessary features and focusing on quick daily interactions. The primary user need was a lightweight tool that makes tracking progress feel manageable rather than overwhelming.

## What screens and features were necessary to support user needs and produce a user-centered UI for the app? How did your UI designs keep users in mind? Why were your designs successful?##

The app includes a main dashboard for entering daily weight data, a progress view for reviewing trends, and a settings screen for managing preferences such as notifications. The UI design emphasizes clarity, simplicity, and consistency by using clearly labeled buttons, minimal navigation paths, and a predictable layout. These design choices helped ensure users could complete tasks quickly without confusion, making the interface effective and user-centered.

## How did you approach the process of coding your app? What techniques or strategies did you use? How could those techniques or strategies be applied in the future? ##

I approached the coding process with a strong goal of planning and structure before moving into implementation. I mapped out activity lifecycles and UI interactions ahead of time and built features in a modular, incremental way, and then tested that each component was developed and functioning before being integrated with others. These techniques can help in the future to reduce debugging efforts and to improve maintainability and reliability.

## How did you test to ensure your code was functional? Why is this process important, and what did it reveal? ##

Testing was performed using the Android Emulator and manual test cases throughout development. Features were tested individually as they were added to ensure correct behavior before moving forward. This process was important for identifying lifecycle issues, UI interaction problems, and logic errors early, which made final integration smoother and more stable.

## Consider the full app design and development process from initial planning to finalization. Where did you have to innovate to overcome a challenge? ##

One challenge was managing application state and permissions while maintaining a smooth user experience. To overcome this, I had to carefully follow Android lifecycle best practices and adjust my design to align with platform constraints. This required adapting my initial plans to ensure the app behaved predictably across different states.

##  In what specific component of your mobile app were you particularly successful in demonstrating your knowledge, skills, and experience? ##

I was particularly successful in integrating user-centered UI design with structured backend logic. The app demonstrates my ability to translate user needs into a clean interface while supporting those interactions with well-organized and maintainable code.
