How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
  Interpreting user needs begins with listening to stakeholders and the clients, identifying what their professional and personal needs are, and translating those into tasks which are actionable, achievable and recognizable. User stories help this process by breaking down needs and wants into small, clear, and manageable chunks while keeping the user’s perspective in mind. This ensures that the development team understands the value of each feature and prioritizes tasks effectively.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
  My method is to take an iterative approach to development, starting small and building the project up while continuously testing and refining. No one gets a process right the first time, so it's important to give yourself room to grow, treating the project like clay instead of marble. In the future I would like to apply Agile processes like daily stand-ups, sprint planning, and retrospectives in order to ensure that my teams collaborate and adapt well. I also value frequent feedback and user reviews to keep the work aligned with user and owner expectations.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
  A good team member most importantly is open with their communication, supports others, and takes ownership of tasks. Focusing on the mission, adapting to change, learning whenever able, and just being positive are also important traits in a professional working environment. It's about balancing individual contributions with team goals and ensuring shared success.
